FIELD 5: Short description — how the idea relates to the category and
         its relevance to HPE Business (max 200 words)
================================================================================

EzAssist is an AI-powered Knowledge Assistant that transforms how HPE Ezmeral
support engineers find and apply troubleshooting information.

Today, engineers spend significant time searching across multiple Knowledge Base
(KB) articles, PDF documents, troubleshooting guides, and Salesforce records to
diagnose customer issues. This increases Mean Time to Resolution (MTTR) and
impacts customer satisfaction.

EzAssist provides a single-stop, natural language search interface where
engineers ask a question and receive consolidated, step-by-step troubleshooting
guidance drawn from multiple knowledge sources. The system uses a Retrieval-
Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture with a locally-hosted LLaMA 2 7B model,
semantic search via PostgreSQL+pgvector, and a multi-stage PDF ingestion
pipeline to deliver context-aware answers grounded exclusively in approved
support documentation.

This directly impacts HPE's support business by:
- Reducing MTTR through instant access to relevant procedures
- Improving engineer productivity by eliminating multi-system searches
- Accelerating onboarding of new support engineers
- Ensuring consistent troubleshooting guidance across global teams
- Continuous knowledge expansion through PDF document upload

FIELD 7: How have you used AI in development of Idea/Submission?
================================================================================

AI is both the core of the solution and was used in its development:

IN THE SOLUTION:
- LLaMA 2 7B (quantized) runs entirely within the Kubernetes pod as the
  generative AI engine that synthesizes answers from retrieved knowledge
- Sentence-Transformers (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) provides AI-powered semantic
  embeddings for intelligent document search — going beyond keyword matching
  to understand the meaning of support queries
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture ensures the AI only
  answers from approved documentation, preventing fabricated responses

IN DEVELOPMENT:
- GitHub Copilot was used extensively for code development, debugging,
  prompt engineering, and deployment automation
- AI-assisted analysis of token usage patterns to optimize the context
  window for the LLM
- AI-assisted drafting and refinement of the anti-hallucination prompt
  framework that ensures answer reliability
